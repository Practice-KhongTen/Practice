Hung Temple Festival

History
According to Ngọc Pha Hùng Vương, who wrote in the reign of Hong Duc after the Le dynasty, from the time of the Đinh, Tien Le, Ly and Tran dynasties, to the Le dynasty, they still had the same incense smoke in the temple. Remember the merits of the ancient Ancestor. Hung King's death anniversary was recognized by feudal dynasties as one of Vietnam's national holidays. Since ancient times, the monarchial and feudal dynasties of Vietnam have managed Hung Temple in a way that is assigned directly to the local people to look after, repair, worship, and commemorate the death anniversary of the 10th lunar month; in return, the locals were exempted from the royalties for field taxes collected and supplemented by soldiers.

Hung temple image

The festival takes place on April 10 of the calendar year, however, the festival actually took place from the previous weeks with customs such as throwing (beating the drum) of the Muong people, pilgrimage to commemorate the kings. Hung, and ends on the 10th of the third lunar month with a procession and incense offering on Thuong temple. The Hung Temple Festival is now raised by the State of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam into a great national death anniversary in even years. There are 2 ceremonies celebrated at the same time as the festival:
Procession of the palanquin: The procession of palanquin, many colors of many flags, flowers, parasols, palanquin, traditional costumes comes from the foot of the mountain and then turns to temples to Thuong temple, the place of making incense offering ceremony.
Incense offering ceremony: Pilgrims go to Hung Temple mainly for the needs of spiritual life. Each person lighted up some incense sticks when he came to the Land of the Patriarch to ask for his smoke to tell his thoughts to his ancestors. According to the concept of Vietnamese people, each handful of soil and stump here is sacred and the stumps and holes are filled with incense. Part Assembly has many folk games. Those were Xoan singing competitions, a special form of folk songs of Phu Tho, wrestling competitions, tug of war, or swimming competitions at the junction of the Bach Hac River, where the Hung Kings practiced Marine infantry training teams
